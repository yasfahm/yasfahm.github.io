Understanding the Problem

Human history is often divided into pre and post industrial revolution, but in the future the distinguishing landmark will probably be the advent and prevalence of computers. Computers have revolutionized the way humans react with their external selves, including other humans. But recently, the adverse effects of computers, and particularly screen time, on human mental and physical health are under increased scrutiny. Over half the population admits excessive use of their smartphones. The problem is particularly grave amongst teens and young-adults, leading to problems like anxiety, poor posture, sleep issues, weight gain, risk of heart disease, and more at perturbingly younger ages. COVID has exacerbated the already serious problem of screen-addiction and excessive screen time. All of these come at the expense of the users’ mental and physical health.


The goal is to design an app that promotes a healthier lifestyle for the adults, while ensuring healthier screen habits. It is a strangely paradoxical task to design an app that the users spend time operating, and yet that aims to reduce the screen time users spend. The challenge grows in the face of covid where screen time often provides the only outlet for users to interact with friends and family, an aspect undoubtedly paramount to better mental health, and one of the few sources of entertainment.

Our Solution

Any and all design solutions should always address the problem of increased screen time. It would be easy to just pack the app with features aimed at promoting a healthier lifestyle, like bombardment of notifications to log activities, track mental health or suggestions like journaling. However, this defeats the purpose as the user must consequently spend an increased amount of time using the app. Instead we aim to identify the absolutely crucial tasks that the users must engage in, which would ensure the highest amount of time away from the screens, and yet would have the highest positive impact on mental and physical health.

QUESTIONNAIRE

The goal of our app is to encourage users to focus on fulfilling basic needs. After gathering the responses, we noticed that most people spend the majority of their time in front of screens for entertainment and work. According to Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs, the foundation tier is physiological such as food, water, sleep, and warmth. Though we had an idea of incorporating these needs as features, it was vital to prove a strong correlation between the users’ necessities and our assumptions. The main features are loosely based on these essentials that most of us neglect daily due to the current state of the world. Click here for our survey questions

INTERVIEWS

The next step of validating our users was to create personas and trying to discover what drives them. Our interview questions were conducted with an audience of 18 people. The proposed questions will be used as segments for the personas. We focused on the goals, frustrations, interests, and ways of improving mental health to envision the types of users for LUMI. To be more inclusive of the preferences among users, we added several other features such as journaling, medicine, and screen time. LUMI will encourage users to customize what makes them happy and will manage all they do to attain that aim with its multiple features. Click here for our interview questions

OUR APP
Account Setup

Upon starting the app, if the user is not signed in, he is redirected to the account setup page. There he has options to either sign in or register with Lumi app, or continue with third-party authentication.


Home Page

After login, the user is redirected to the home page. There the user has a few features that he has subscribed to. On the same page, he has the option to drag and drop additional “add-on” features to the “current features” list. By clicking on any of these features, the user is directed to the feature page.


Water Intake

The main page shows the water consumed in the day. The user has the option to set the goals and frequency with which they aim to consume water. The screen has two buttons; one leads to the add-water page where the user can record the water he has consumed. The other allows him to see the water report tracked through the past 7 days.


Exercise

The main page has a few workout routines that the user can choose from. Each routine leads to a separate page with a list of exercises. Clicking on any of these exercises leads to the exercise page where the correct form is explained and tips for maximum effectiveness are provided.


Sleep

The main page has two buttons; Sleep which starts the timer and is disabled when the user wakes up and starts using the app again. Second button is Add Sleep which allows the user to log in any sleep that they may have had. There’s also a calendar, from where the user can go to the past days.
Clicking on the past days leads to the statistics page. It has several buttons, four for viewing the reports and two for adding non-quantifiable data like restedness and fatigueness.


Nutrition

The main page has a meter that indicates how much of the daily calories have been consumed. On the same page are options to add meals and adjust the macros of the meals consumed. Also included are options to view the nutrition reports from the past week.
The add meal button leads to the add meal page where the user can search for foods, which already have calories and macro values assigned to them. Users can also add custom food and add their own calories and macro values.


Journaling

The main page has two buttons: day and night. Both lead to their corresponding sections where the user can answer some questions about their day. The questions chosen are the ones scientifically proven to provide the most mental benefit.


Medication

The medication main page has a list and times of medicine to be taken on that day. There are buttons to add more medicines to be taken, and a button to view the weekly report of medicine taken.


Meditation

The main page has a list of meditation categories. Each leads to a page where the user is guided through a meditation with the aid of a video. Also included are tips to help them get the most out of their session.


Screen Time

The main page has an indicator of the screen-time quota already met out of the set goal amount. Also included is a list of apps where most screen-time was spent. Lastly, there are two buttons. One leads to the weekly report page, and the other to the friends’ activity page.
The users have the option to enable sharing their screen-time activity with their friends. If enabled, their screen-time appears on this page. The hall of fame has the users with least weekly screen time, whereas the hall of fame has friends with the most.

User Flow

The user flow was a useful visual representation that we created to answer both logical and design concerns. Failed Designs

Our first idea was to use pastel colors which is a UI/UX trend in 2021. After multiple brainstorming sessions, we thought it would be a good idea to use different colors for each feature (blue for water, green for nutrition, yellow for sleep, red for exercise, etc). However, after starting the prototype, we realized that having multiple colors on our app will be overwhelming for the user. Therefore, we opted for a minimalistic color palette where we used blue/cyan as accent colors.

Conclusion

When designing LUMI, our main goal was to create an app that can be personalized by the user. We wanted to have some features common for every user as well as features that will be specific to each one. Finding the basic features was an easy process but choosing other features that may differ from one account to another was a challenge. Each of our features encourage the user to focus on their mental and physical health whether it's by offering a journaling section, tracking calories/water intake/sleep, exercising and/or meditating, reminding them to take their medication and setting a screen time limit. Throughout this process, we discovered that each one of us had different definitions of a healthy lifestyle. Thus, trying to find a balance between each of our ideas was also challenging.
